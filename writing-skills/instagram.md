# Instagram Writing Pattern

Instagram is visual-first. The image, carousel, Reel, or Story carries the main experience. The caption supports the media; it should not read like a Facebook post under an image.

## Adaptation

- Make the persona more visual, concise, and emotionally clear.
- Keep one core idea from the template.
- Move extra explanation into carousel slides, video, comments, or the linked blog.
- Use a strong first line because long captions are collapsed.
- Use 1-2 relevant emojis total when they fit the subject and persona.
- Default to 0-3 relevant hashtags, not a generic hashtag block.

## Length

- Feed image: 1-3 short sentences, 20-60 words
- Carousel caption: 1-3 short sentences, 20-50 words
- Reel caption: 1-2 short sentences, 10-35 words
- Story text: one short action or thought

## Patterns

Feed image:

```text
[Visual-aware hook]
[One core story or insight]
[CTA or short supporting detail]
[0-3 relevant hashtags]
```

Carousel:

```text
Caption:
[Promise or outcome]
[Why this matters]
[Save/share/comment CTA]
[0-3 relevant hashtags]

Slide logic:
1. Hook
2. Problem/context
3. Insight
4. Example/proof
5. Takeaway/CTA
```

Reel:

```text
[Short hook tied to video]
[One context sentence]
[Action CTA]
[0-3 relevant hashtags]
```

Story:

```text
[Very short overlay]
[Sticker/action prompt]
```

## Check

- First line works before "more."
- Caption adds meaning to the media.
- Caption is no more than 3 short sentences.
- Emojis are relevant and limited to 1-2 total.
- CTA fits Instagram behavior: save, share, comment, DM, link in bio.
- Hashtags are relevant and limited.
