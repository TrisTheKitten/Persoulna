# Facebook Writing Pattern

Facebook is for context, trust, community, links, events, and longer explanation. Keep it human and readable; do not turn it into a wall of text.

## Adaptation

- Keep more of the user's writing template than other platforms.
- Make the persona feel relational and community-aware.
- Use short paragraphs.
- Put the main point in the first 1-2 lines.
- Use few or no hashtags unless the brand intentionally uses them.

## Length

- Short update: 60-120 words
- Launch/story: 120-250 words
- Educational post: 150-350 words

## Patterns

Community update:

```text
[Warm hook]
[What happened / what is changing]
[Why it matters to the audience]
[Simple conversational CTA]
```

Educational post:

```text
[Problem, belief, or observation]
[Short explanation]
[2-4 practical points]
[Question or link CTA]
```

Launch or offer:

```text
[Clear announcement]
[Who it helps]
[Main benefit or proof]
[How to take action]
```

## Check

- First line makes the value clear.
- Context is present but not bloated.
- CTA feels conversational.
- Link, image, or offer is easy to notice.
